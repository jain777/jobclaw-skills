# Negotiation leverage rubric

How `coach-negotiation` weighs the levers before drafting a counter. Score each lever `strong / medium / weak / none`.

## Strong leverage
- **Competing offer in hand** — same level, similar or higher comp. Single biggest factor.
- **Recruiter-initiated outreach** — you didn't apply; they came to you. Implies budget flexibility.
- **Niche / verified skill match** — JD asks land precisely on the user's profile (a handful of people fit). Cite the JD line.
- **Time pressure on the company** — multiple offers in the role, urgent hiring (visible in news / Glassdoor / Blind chatter).

## Medium leverage
- **Tier-1 alma-mater / brand-name prior** — measurable but waning fast with experience.
- **Niche domain experience** — payments, healthcare AI, defense, etc., where talent supply is genuinely thin.
- **Recent positive company news** — fundraise (cash leverage), product win, expansion (headcount pressure).

## Weak leverage
- "I really want this role" — emotional, no signal.
- "The market pays X" — without a specific source.
- Generic credentials (CS degree, common framework experience).

## No / negative leverage
- Already verbally accepted without conditioning ("yes, send the paperwork") — restart with care; the move is for "scope clarity" not "renegotiation."
- Tight timeline *on your side* — needing to close fast.
- Long-tenure gap or recency issues you haven't addressed.
- Visa / sponsorship dependency in a "no-sponsorship" listing.

## How to use it
1. Score each lever for this offer.
2. The 1–2 strongest levers anchor the counter email's reasoning. Mention them lightly — never dump all of them in one email.
3. Medium leverage justifies the target number internally; usually unspoken in the email.
4. Weak / none leverage = don't surface; rely on market data.

## Region notes
- **US:** equity is the most negotiable component; base less so at large public cos. Signing bonus is the easiest "yes." Pay-range disclosure laws give you the room to ask numerically.
- **IN:** **fixed CTC** is the main lever; variable / ESOPs are softer asks. **Notice period** is part of the package — short notice (15–30 days) is bargaining value to many employers; long notice can soften offers.
